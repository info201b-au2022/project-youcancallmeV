Project Dynamic

Using a compilation of data originating from a wide array of American colleges that was compiled by Samson Qian of MIT, we first arranged a table of specific data points by state, and locating the average values for each state’s average undergraduate population, admission rate, SAT submission rate, and cost of both out-of-state and in-state attendance. The data averages vary wildly from state to state with Nevada having a significantly higher average undergraduate population than other states such as Alaska. Similarly, Wyoming also eclipses Alaska in average admittance rate by double digit percentage points. Both of these could be due to the relatively low populations and amount of colleges located in these states.

After generating a table of averages, we created charts detailing a more precise examination of SAT scores and tuition rates. In contrast to the states with the highest admittance rates and undergraduate populations, the state with the highest average SAT scores of accepted students, Delaware is in a much denser population area, which may contribute to their position relative to other states on this particular data point. Similarly, the state with the highest average in-state tuition, District of Columbia, and highest average out-of-state tuition, also District of Columbia, is notable as a distinctly populous location, that also boasts a high rate or urbanization, as well as a higher population of citizens in a more affluent relative wealth bracket.
